Why I Started FrankPass

It started with a phone call.

A close friend of mine lost his entire bank savings because a hacker performed a SIM-swap on his phone number, intercepted his 2FA codes, and drained his account in under 15 minutes. 15 years of hard-earned money, gone in 15 minutes.

That night, I couldn't sleep. I kept thinking: "How is it possible that in 2025, the entire digital life of a human being depends on a single password stored in someone else's cloud?"

I started researching. I learned about password vaults getting hacked (LastPass, 2022). I learned about credential stuffing attacks that exploit reused passwords. And I realized the fundamental flaw in the system:

The problem is not weak passwords. The problem is trusting someone else to protect them for you.

So I built FrankPass — a tool where your password is never stored anywhere. It is generated on the fly using pure mathematics, right inside your browser. No server, no cloud, no database. If there is nothing to steal, there is nothing to hack.

I believe that core security should be a human right, not a subscription service. That's why the FrankPass website will always be free for everyone.

My Philosophy

I don't believe in "trust us, we're secure." I believe in "we can't betray your trust even if we wanted to."

FrankPass is designed so that even I, as the developer, cannot see your passwords. The math runs entirely on your device. I have no access to your inputs, your outputs, or anything in between.

This is called Zero-Knowledge Architecture — and it's the gold standard in privacy engineering.
