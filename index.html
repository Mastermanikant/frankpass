Complete Guide: How to Use FrankPass

Remember just 1 personal phrase → Generate unique, unbreakable passwords for every website, forever.

This tool is 100% stateless. It saves nothing anywhere. Enter the same details → get the exact same password every time. Like a calculator: same input = same output.

📍 1. Country / Region

Select your country. This does two things:

  • Personalizes the platform suggestions — e.g., if you select India, you'll see Paytm, PhonePe, JioMart.
  • Does NOT affect your password. You can change your country anytime without changing your passwords.

🌐 2. Platform / Website

Type the name of the website or app you need a password for. Examples: Facebook, Gmail, Amazon.

Auto-Suggest: As you type, FrankPass suggests popular platforms. For example, type fa and Facebook appears — just select it. This prevents spelling mistakes.

Don't worry about format: Whether you type facebook, Facebook, FACEBOOK, facebook.com, www.facebook.com, or m.facebook.com — FrankPass automatically normalizes it to the same base name. You will always get the same password.

Platform not listed? If a platform is missing from auto-suggest, just type it manually. It will still work perfectly. If you want us to add it to the auto-suggest list, tag @FrankPass on X (Twitter) or Instagram with your request, or email us — we'll add it as soon as possible.

👤 3. Username / Email (Optional)

This field is optional. Our recommendation: leave it blank.

Why? If you change your email on a website later, the generated password would change too (because the input changed). Leaving it blank keeps things simple.

When to use it: Only when you have multiple accounts on the same platform. For example, if you have 2 Instagram accounts, enter the username for each to get different passwords.

🔐 4. Secret Personal Key

This is the most important field. It's your personal master phrase — the mathematical seed for all your passwords.

What to enter: Any phrase that only you know and will never forget. Examples:

  • 2friends+mango+2026
  • mydog$lucky$birthday
  • papa-ki-dukaan-1995

Does complexity matter? No! Whether your key is simple or complex, the generated password will always be cryptographically strong (1 million hash iterations). Your key is just the seed — the math does the rest.

Toggle 👁️: Use the toggle switch to show/hide your key while typing.

🔄 5. Variant Counter (Optional)

Default is 1. This is your password rotation tool.

When to change it:

  • A website forces you to change your password → Change variant from 1 to 2.
  • You suspect your account was compromised → Change to the next number.

Security value: Even if someone discovers your Secret Key, they still can't generate your password without knowing which Variant number you used. This adds an extra layer of protection.

⚙️ 6. Profile

  • Standard (Recommended) — Letters + Numbers + Symbols. Maximum security.
  • Alphanumeric — Letters + Numbers only. Use for websites that don't allow symbols.
  • Numeric (PIN) — Numbers only. For banking PINs or numeric-only fields.

📏 7. Password Length

Slide between 8 and 64 characters. Recommended: 16+ for maximum security. Some old websites limit to 16 characters — adjust as needed.

🔑 8. Generated Password

Your password is generated and automatically copied to your clipboard. Security timers:

  • Password auto-clears in 15 seconds — so no one can see it on your screen.
  • Secret Key auto-clears after 30 seconds of inactivity — if you stop using the page.
  • Closing the tab instantly destroys all data from memory.

🛡️ 9. About Data Leaks

From FrankPass's side, a data leak is impossible. We have no database, no server storage, and no cloud. Neither we, nor anyone else in the world, can ever know or see the passwords you generate. The math runs entirely inside your browser.

However, the platforms you use (like Facebook, Amazon, etc.) can still suffer data breaches on their end. To stay informed:

  • Follow FrankPass on social media — we share major breach alerts as fast as possible.
  • Personalized Alerts (Coming Soon): Join FrankBase Digital's free personalized security alert service. Our smart AI monitors Google Alerts, Dark Web feeds, and AI-powered data scraping to detect breaches. When a breach is detected, our AI sends a detailed report to our team, who verifies it across multiple layers. If verification takes time, you'll receive an immediate warning: "This data might be leaked — please change your password immediately." This service may become paid in the future.

सुरक्षा लाभ: अगर किसी को आपकी Secret Key भी पता चल जाए, तो बिना Variant नंबर जाने वो आपका सही पासवर्ड नहीं बना सकता। यह एक extra सुरक्षा परत है।

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Security Architecture of FrankPass?
A: FrankPass uses 100% client-side cryptography. Advanced hashing algorithms (PBKDF2 and HMAC-SHA512 with 1,000,000 iterations) run locally in your browser to generate deterministic output. Because there are no databases, vaults, or cloud syncing involved, there is nothing for hackers to breach. It is completely serverless.
What happens if I forget my Secret Key?
A: Because FrankPass is zero-knowledge and stateless, there is no "Forgot Password" or recovery feature. Your Secret Key is the mathematical foundation of your passwords. We recommend using a memorable passphrase or your deeply ingrained personal logic. If you forget it, you will have to reset your passwords on every individual website.
Will I get the same password if I switch devices?
A: Absolutely. FrankPass is deterministic. As long as you input the exact same 'Platform Name', 'Secret Key', and 'Variant Counter', the math will output the exact same generated password on any smartphone, tablet, PC, or Mac, entirely offline.
What if I type "facebook.com" instead of just "facebook"? Will my password change?
A: Good news! FrankPass has a built-in smart domain normalizer. Whether you type facebook, facebook.com, www.facebook.com, or even m.facebook.com, our system automatically cleans it up to just use the base name (facebook). So yes, you will get the exact same password without worrying about complex URLs!
Does FrankPass work completely offline?
A: Yes! Once the page is loaded, you can completely disconnect your internet. The cryptography functions natively inside your browser. You can even save the HTML file to your desktop and run it locally forever for ultimate paranoia-level security.
Why should I leave the "Username" field blank?
A: We suggest leaving it blank to reduce 'Human Error'. If you change your email address on a website, configuring the generator with your new email would result in a different password, locking you out. Leaving it blank keeps the password tied purely to the platform and your secret key. However, if you have multiple accounts on the same platform (like two Instagram profiles), you must use the Username field to generate distinctly different passwords for each.
What if a website rejects the generated password?
A: Some aging websites have strict legacy rules, like limiting passwords to 16 characters or restricting certain symbols. If your password is rejected, simply lower the length slider or change the Profile to 'Alphanumeric' and generate again. Remember to always use those same settings for that specific website in the future.
How does the Variant counter feature work?
A: Think of the variant as a password rotation tool. If a website asks you to change your password every 90 days, or if you suspect your account was compromised, simply change the variant from "1" (default) to "2" to generate a brand new, secure password for that same platform, without changing your master key.
Why do the passwords look slightly different from other generators?
A: For your sanity! We strategically removed "ambiguous" characters like the letter l, the number 1, the capital letter I, the letter O, and the number 0 from our engine. This means you will never misread a password generated by FrankPass when typing it on another device.

FrankPass is 100% Stateless and Zero-Knowledge. We don't have a database, which means we cannot collect your email or alert you when websites get hacked.
