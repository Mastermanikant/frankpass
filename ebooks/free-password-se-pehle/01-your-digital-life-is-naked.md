## The Numbers Don't Lie

In 2024 alone, over **22 billion records** were leaked in data breaches worldwide. That's roughly three passwords exposed for every single human being on Earth.

Here are some breaches you've probably heard of:

- **Facebook (2021):** 533 million users' phone numbers, emails, and locations leaked. Posted online for free.
- **Yahoo (2013–2016):** All 3 billion accounts were breached. Every. Single. One.
- **Aadhaar (2023):** Over 815 million Indian citizens' biometric and identity data leaked on the dark web for $80.
- **LinkedIn (2021):** 700 million users' data scraped and sold.

These aren't small companies with weak security teams. These are billion-dollar corporations with thousands of engineers. If they can't keep your data safe, who can?

**Answer: Only you.**

## "I Have Nothing to Hide"

This is the most dangerous sentence in cybersecurity.

People say it all the time. "I'm not important. Hackers won't come after me. I have nothing worth stealing."

Let's test that.

Do you have:
- A bank account linked to your phone number?
- An email address that's the same one you use for password resets?
- A social media profile with your real name and city?
- A UPI ID that's your phone number?

If yes, you have **plenty** worth stealing.

Here's what a hacker can do with just your email and phone number:

1. **Reset your Instagram password** (they just need access to your email).
2. **Clone your SIM card** and intercept your OTPs.
3. **Access your bank** through UPI using your phone number.
4. **Open a fake loan** in your name using your Aadhaar details from a leaked database.

This isn't theory. This happens to thousands of ordinary people every single day. In India, UPI fraud cases crossed ₹14,000 crore in 2023. Most victims were regular people who thought they had "nothing to hide."

## The Real Cost

When someone steals your identity, it's not just about money. It's:

- **Time:** Weeks spent on police reports, bank visits, and verification processes.
- **Reputation:** Someone sending messages from your hacked Instagram to your contacts.
- **Mental health:** The constant anxiety of not knowing what else they have access to.
- **Trust:** Your family and friends receiving scam messages "from you."

One college student in Delhi had her Instagram hacked in 2024. The hacker sent messages to all her contacts asking for money via UPI. Her best friend sent ₹5,000 before realizing it was a scam. It took three months to recover the account. The friendship? That took longer.

The truth is, digital security isn't complicated. It's just poorly explained.
